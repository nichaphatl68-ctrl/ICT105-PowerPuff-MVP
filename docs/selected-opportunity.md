# Selected Semester Project Opportunity

## Selected Project Title
Campus Smart Room Booking System

## Target User
University students, student clubs, and campus organizations who need reliable access to academic study spaces and meeting rooms.

## Problem Statement
Students often waste time walking to administrative offices to check room availability and make bookings manually, which frequently leads to frustrating scheduling conflicts, double-bookings, and academic delays.

## Current Alternative
Students currently have to check space/room availability in person. If the desired space or room is already occupied, they must wander campus to find a new one because they have no way of knowing whether a room is full beforehand.

## Proposed IT Solution
A responsive web application featuring:
* **Frontend:** Built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
* **Backend:** Powered by Node.js / Express.
* **Database:** Structured using MySQL for reliable relational scheduling data.
* **Authentication:** A secure University email login system to verify student identities.
* **Hosting:** Deployed and hosted on Firebase for rapid development and accessibility.

## Why This Opportunity Was Selected
Using the NUF scoring framework, this project scored exceptionally high across all fields:
* **New:** It transitions a slow, outdated manual walk-in system into an instant, digital hub.
* **Useful:** It solves a daily, universal friction point for every student trying to study or run club meetings on campus.
* **Feasible:** The scope is highly manageable for a student engineering team within a single semester.

## Why This Is Feasible Without IoT or Advanced Cybersecurity
This project functions entirely as a software-driven coordination tool rather than relying on automated physical hardware. Instead of expensive IoT door locks or complex physical sensors, room validation can be managed effectively through software-generated check-in states or digital codes. Basic web security protocols (like encrypted session handling and standard database access controls) are more than sufficient to safely isolate user accounts without requiring industrial-grade cybersecurity infrastructure.

## Possible MVP Type
Simple web app prototype.

## Initial Validation Plan for Lab 03
* **Who will the team ask?** We will interview a sample of 10–15 university students and at least 1-2 campus facility staff members who manage room keys or permissions.
* **What evidence will the team collect?** We will collect data on typical room booking wait times, the average number of weekly scheduling conflicts students experience, and user feedback on our initial wireframe navigation flow.
* **What question must be answered first?** "Will both students and faculty trust a digital schedule enough to stop showing up physically to claim rooms, and does our database logic effectively prevent concurrent double-bookings?"
